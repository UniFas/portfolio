Horror Design Case Studies

Analyzing iconic horror games through a system-design viewpoint.

Layers of Fear (2016)

Layers of Fear taught me how environmental manipulation can replace enemy encounters. Its “designer controlled movement window” offered maximum tension without combat, and its psychological horror loop relies on misdirection, controlled pacing, and player-driven interpretation.

Key insights included perspective based scares, non-linear spatial loops, and mood-driven setpieces. These systems inspired how BTQ handles dread without revealing the monster too early.

SOMA (2015)

SOMA showed me the power of narrative-driven horror systems. The game uses “existential friction” instead of jumpscares, letting mechanics reinforce philosophical themes.

Its monster AI supports the narrative rather than dominating it perfect inspiration for BTQ's psychological tension loops and non-combat fear-driven progression.

Lucius (2012)

Lucius explores systemic mischief, where the player manipulates NPCs through item based chains. Studying it taught me how systems can reinforce theme even when the player is the threat.

I drew inspiration for BTQ’s environmental storytelling and “fear escalation” through logical events instead of combat.
